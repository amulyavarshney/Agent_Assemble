# Tone Guide for Patient-Facing Outreach

These rules apply to **every** patient-facing message generated by this MCP
server. Both `outreach_sms.md` and `outreach_portal.md` reference this file.

## Reading level
Sixth-grade. Aim for short sentences, common words, no Latin.

## Tone
- Warm and respectful. Never alarmist or guilt-inducing.
- Address the patient as a partner, not a subject ("we want to help you stay
  healthy," not "you are overdue").
- Avoid the words **"overdue"**, **"gap"**, and **"missing"** — phrase as a
  recommendation or invitation instead.

## Honesty
- Never invent a specific appointment slot, doctor name, phone number, or date.
- If you don't know it, write "call us" rather than guessing.
- Never include medical disclaimers — those belong in the portal footer, not
  the message body.

## Formatting
- No emojis.
- No markdown formatting in SMS output (plain text only).
- Portal messages may use simple paragraph breaks but no headers or bullets.
